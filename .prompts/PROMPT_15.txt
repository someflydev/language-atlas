You are an expert technical educator and curriculum designer. Your task is to design "Learning Paths" for the "language-atlas" project.

CONTEXT:
The "language-atlas" is designed for future learners in the AI era. Rather than just listing languages, we want to prescribe paths through the atlas to teach specific conceptual shifts (e.g., "From OOP to FP", "Understanding Memory Safety").

TASK:
1. Update `data/learning_paths.json` with comprehensive path data.
2. Define at least 4 "Learning Paths" (educational) and 2 "Industry Discovery Projects" (research-oriented).
   - Use the standardized generations (`dawn` through `autonomic`) as a conceptual guide.
3. Each entry in `data/learning_paths.json` should have:
   - `id`, `title`, `description`, `type` (learning_path or industry_discovery).
   - `target_audience`.
   - `steps`: An ordered array of concepts or languages to visit, with a rationale for *why* that step is there.
4. Update `app.py` and templates to expose a `/paths` route displaying these learning and research progressions.

CONSTRAINTS & REQUIREMENTS:
- Example Path 1: "The Systems Descent" (Java -> C -> Rust). Rationale: Understand garbage collection, then manual memory, then borrow checking.

- Example Industry Project: "The Space-Grade Constraint" (Ada -> C -> Zig). Rationale: Research why safety-critical systems prioritize determinism and what modern systems languages offer.

- Integrate the UI using the same HTMX + Jinja2 stack. The UI should render the path clearly, linking to the relevant language profiles (in `docs/LANGUAGE_PROFILES/`) or concept docs.
