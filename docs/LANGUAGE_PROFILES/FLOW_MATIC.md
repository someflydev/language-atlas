# FLOW-MATIC: The Mother of COBOL

## Overview
FLOW-MATIC (originally known as B-0) was the first English-like data-processing language. It was designed to allow business managers and non-mathematicians to describe data processing tasks in a language that resembled natural English sentences.

## Historical Context
Created by Grace Hopper at Remington Rand in 1955, FLOW-MATIC was a reaction to the math-heavy focus of early languages like FORTRAN. Hopper believed that for computers to be useful in business, they had to speak the language of the office, not the laboratory. It became the primary inspiration for the development of COBOL.

## Mental Model
To program in FLOW-MATIC, you must **think in terms of English commands**. You aren't writing formulas; you are writing instructions for a clerk to follow.

Your brain must be wired to:
1. **Use Verbs and Nouns:** Statements take the form of "ADD PRICE TO TOTAL" or "MOVE RECORD TO FILE."
2. **Focus on Records:** Data is organized into "files" and "records," mirroring the physical filing systems of 1950s businesses.
3. **Embrace Readability:** The goal is that a business supervisor should be able to read the program and understand the logic without being a technical expert.

## Key Innovations
- **English-Like Syntax:** Replacing mathematical symbols with English words to make programming more accessible.
- **Separation of Data and Logic:** One of the first languages to explicitly separate the description of the data from the procedures that act upon it.
- **Compiler Pioneer:** Hopper's work on FLOW-MATIC helped establish the viability of high-level compilers for business applications.

## Tradeoffs & Criticisms
- **Verbosity:** Writing even simple programs required a significant amount of typing compared to more symbolic languages.
- **Limited Scope:** It was strictly designed for business data processing and lacked the mathematical power needed for scientific or engineering work.
- **Hardware Lock-in:** FLOW-MATIC was tied to the UNIVAC computers, limiting its broader adoption before the standardized COBOL was released.

## Legacy
FLOW-MATIC's greatest legacy is **COBOL**. The idea that code should be readable by humans and focused on business data remains a cornerstone of enterprise computing. Grace Hopper's vision for "English-language programming" changed the course of the software industry forever.
